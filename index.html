A Data Giant at a Crossroads: Strong Foundations, Stretched Valuation

Snowflake has built one of the most strategically important platforms in enterprise software — a cloud-agnostic data layer that sits beneath analytics, AI, and application workloads. With net revenue retention above 125%, RPO growth around 40%, and product gross margins near 74%, the company's unit economics remain best-in-class. The recent push into Cortex AI, Snowpark, and native applications expands the platform's addressable market considerably. Yet at ~21× forward revenue, we believe the current share price already embeds much of this optionality. Our DCF-derived fair value of roughly $252 leaves slim upside from $242, supporting a HOLD recommendation until the risk-reward improves.

Risk Factors
What Could Derail the Thesis
Snowflake's premium valuation leaves little room for disappointment. Several structural and cyclical risks could compress the multiple quickly.

Hyperscaler Competition

AWS, Azure, and GCP are building native analytics and AI services. If they close the feature gap or undercut on pricing, Snowflake's win rate could decline.

Consumption Slowdown

Usage-based revenue cuts both ways. In a macro downturn, enterprises optimize cloud spend — directly hitting Snowflake's top line.

NRR Compression

A drop below 120% net retention would signal weakening expansion and likely trigger a rapid multiple de-rating from current levels.

Stock-Based Compensation

Elevated SBC dilutes shareholders and keeps GAAP profitability negative. Until this normalizes, valuation debates will persist.
