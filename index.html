Risk Factors
What Could Go Wrong
While Snowflake's positioning is strong, several factors could pressure the stock and justify caution at current valuation levels.

Hyperscaler Competition

AWS, Azure, and GCP continue to build native analytics and AI services. If hyperscalers undercut Snowflake on price or match its features, adoption could slow.

Consumption Deceleration

In a macro slowdown, enterprises may optimize cloud spend. Since Snowflake's revenue is usage-based, a pullback in data processing volumes directly impacts revenue.

NRR Compression

If net revenue retention falls below 120%, it would signal weakening customer expansion and likely trigger a significant multiple de-rating.

Stock-Based Compensation

Snowflake's SBC remains elevated relative to revenue, diluting shareholders. Until GAAP profitability improves, the valuation premium could come under pressure.
